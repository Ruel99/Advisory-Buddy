Course Overview

Academic Writing

Course Code: ENGL125

Course Description:

Academic Writing is a college-level course focused on developing advanced skills in scholarly communication. Students learn to compose and articulate well-researched, structured essays while mastering academic conventions and citation styles.

Pre-Requisite(s): CXC ENGLISH

Computer Science I

Course Code: CPTR150

Course Description:

Computer Science 1 is an introductory college course providing a foundational understanding of key concepts in computing. Students explore programming fundamentals, algorithmic thinking, and problem-solving skills, laying the groundwork for further studies in computer science.

Pre-Requisite(s): CPTR120 & MATH182


Philosophy of Service

Course Code: BHSC100

Course Description:

Course Description: The Philosophy of Service course delves into the ethical foundations and conceptual frameworks underpinning the principles of service in various contexts, examining the philosophical dimensions of altruism, social responsibility, and the moral obligations inherent in serving others.

Pre-Requisite(s): None Needed

Principles of Macroeconomics

Course Code: ECON277

Course Description:

Course Description: Principles of Macroeconomics explores the fundamental concepts and theories shaping national economies, covering topics such as GDP, unemployment, inflation, and fiscal policy. This course equips students with a comprehensive understanding of macroeconomic principles essential for analyzing and influencing economic systems.

Pre-Requisite(s): MATH165


Web Development

Course Code: CPTR331

Course Description:

Course Description: Web Development is a comprehensive course that guides students through the principles and practical skills necessary to design, build, and deploy dynamic websites and web applications. Topics include HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and frameworks, empowering learners to create modern and responsive digital experiences.

Pre-Requisite(s): CPTR 150

Computer Science II

Course Code: CPTR 250

Course Description:

Computer Science II builds upon foundational programming concepts by delving into advanced topics such as data structures, algorithms, and object-oriented programming. Students gain hands-on experience implementing and manipulating complex data structures, while also refining their problem-solving skills through more sophisticated programming assignments

Pre-Requisite(s): CPTR 150


Calculus I

Course Code: MATH 141

Course Description:

Calculus 1 introduces students to fundamental concepts of differential and integral calculus. The course covers topics such as limits, derivatives, and applications of derivatives in rates of change and optimization.

Pre-Requisite(s): CSEC Math Grade I or GCE Add Math

Calculus With Applications

Course Code: MATH 182

Course Description:

Calculus with Applications extends traditional calculus concepts to real-world scenarios, emphasizing practical problem-solving. Topics include the applications of derivatives in modeling rates of change, optimization, and curve sketching, as well as the use of integrals for calculating areas, volumes, and solving problems in physics.

Pre-Requisite(s): MATH 141
